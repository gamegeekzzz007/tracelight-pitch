The APAC Playbook: Volume x Velocity

APAC naturally will be on Tracelight’s target list soon but I am here trying to make a case why now is a good time for English first APAC places like India & Singapore. Hong Kong, Australia & New Zealand are good to have.

1. INDIA
  • The GCC Explosion: As of late 2025, India is home to ~53% of the world’s Global Capability Centers (GCCs). These are no longer just call centers. Major banks (JPMC, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley) have moved sophisticated "middle office" roles here. There are over 1.9 million professionals in these centers, many of whom are analysts whose primary job is grinding through Excel models.
  • The "Agentic" Budget: 58% of Indian GCCs have explicitly budgeted for Agentic AI in 2026 to optimize their 2,000+ analyst armies.
  • The Efficiency Arbitrage: An analyst here costs ~$7,750/year (₹6-7 Lakh). At Kroll, we had entire teams just for formatting & templating. If a $40/month AI can outperform a human "formatting team," the adoption will be immediate and massive.
2. SINGAPORE
  • The "Switzerland of Asia": A boom in Single Family Offices managing $100M+ AUM with tiny teams (3-5 people). They cannot build internal tech; they need Tracelight to act as the "AI Analyst" multiplier.
  • The Check Signers: Most global PE firms (KKR, Blackstone, Warburg Pincus) and Investment Banks have their APAC Investment Committees sitting in Singapore. Even if the grunt work happens elsewhere, the budget approval happens in Singapore. By targeting Singapore, you aren't just selling software; you are getting Tracelight onto the screens of the people who actually sign the deals (and the checks).
  • The Government Catalyst: Grants like PSG (Productivity Solutions Grant) subsidize up to 50% of IT costs. If we get accredited, the government effectively pays half our customer's bill.
3. There’s almost no competition
  • Companies like superjoin and daloopa (competitors of tracelight based out of India) are still building their excel add in and is nowhere near on how tracelight works.
  • While major companies like databricks and Alphasense (acquirer of carousel) have their sales offices in Singapore – but the market is too early for everyone
  • Based on the user survey I did for buttercell – Top IBs and Investment research firms are unaware about the capabilities of a spreadsheet AI – in simple terms they used Microsoft co-pilot and gave up with no hope.
  • This region is screaming for help there is sadly no one selling to them.
